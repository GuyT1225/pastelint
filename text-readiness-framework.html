Framework

The Text Readiness Framework

Prepare text before it leaves your browser. Clean it, rewrite it, prepare it, then publish, speak, document, or analyze it.

Clean / Rewrite / Prepare / Publish

Move from raw text to ready text.

  1. Raw text — copied, generated, drafted, exported, or pasted text.
  2. Clean — remove formatting issues, artifacts, hidden characters, and noise.
  3. Rewrite — improve clarity, tone, rhythm, and flow without changing meaning.
  4. Prepare — shape text for a destination: publishing, speech, documentation, accessibility, or analysis.
  5. Publish — deliver text to readers, listeners, teammates, customers, or tools.

Why readiness matters

Generated text is not automatically ready.

Formatting artifacts, weak phrasing, readability issues, narration problems, and accessibility barriers often remain after text is copied, exported, generated, or drafted.

Text readiness is the process of preparing content for real-world use before it leaves your browser.

Read the Text Preparation Journal for field notes on specific cleanup problems.

How PasteLint fits

One browser-only workflow, three core tools.

  • PasteLint Clean — clean formatting problems, spacing issues, hidden characters, and paste artifacts.
  • SecondDraft — rewrite cleaned text for clearer tone, rhythm, and flow.
  • SSML Builder — prepare approved text for speech, TTS, IVR, and Amazon Polly.

Principle

Prepare text before it leaves your browser.

PasteLint is not an AI detector, humanizer, or bypass tool. It is a local-first preparation workflow for making copied, generated, drafted, or exported text easier to review, revise, and use.
