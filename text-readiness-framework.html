The Text Readiness Framework

Prepare text before it leaves your browser. Clean it, rewrite it, prepare it, then publish, speak, document, or analyze it.

Clean -> Rewrite -> Prepare -> Publish

  1. Raw text — Copied, generated, drafted, exported, or pasted text.
  2. Clean — Remove formatting issues, artifacts, hidden characters, and noise.
  3. Rewrite — Improve clarity, tone, rhythm, and flow without changing meaning.
  4. Prepare — Shape text for a destination: sending, publishing, speech, documentation, accessibility, or analysis.
  5. Publish — Deliver text to readers, listeners, teammates, customers, or tools.

Why readiness matters

Generated text is not automatically ready for publication. Formatting artifacts, weak phrasing, readability issues, narration problems, and accessibility barriers often remain.

Text readiness is the process of preparing content for real-world use.
